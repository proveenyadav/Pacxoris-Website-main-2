Why radiology is broken

Imaging volumes are rising. Radiologist capacity isn't.

CT and MRI scan volumes have grown 10–12x. Radiologists spend 64% of their time on administrative tasks instead of reading scans. Nearly 37% of all CT scans are normal and could be triaged in seconds. The gap between demand and capacity isn't a people problem. It's an infrastructure problem.

Reporting delays hurt patients

Long turnaround times don't just slow operations. They delay diagnoses, complicate medico-legal cases and erode trust.

Fragmented systems, fragmented care

Disconnected RIS, PACS and HIS create data silos. Radiologists work blind without prior scans or context.

Quality inconsistency under pressure

Manual workflows and lack of templates create reporting variability when volume increases.
